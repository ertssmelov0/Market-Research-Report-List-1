<p><p>Terahertz and Infrared Spectroscopy are analytical techniques used to characterize materials by measuring their interaction with electromagnetic radiation in the terahertz and infrared frequency ranges. Terahertz spectroscopy operates in the 0.1 to 10 THz range and is effective for non-destructive testing, allowing for the identification of materials, detection of hidden objects, and studying molecular dynamics. Infrared spectroscopy, on the other hand, focuses on the 0.4 to 400 THz range and is widely used in chemical analysis, quality control, and material science, focusing on molecular vibrations and functional groups.</p><p>The Terahertz and Infrared Spectroscopy Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 14.9% during the forecast period, driven by the rising demand in various sectors including pharmaceuticals, environmental monitoring, and food safety.
